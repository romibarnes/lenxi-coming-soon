We launch Monday

I designed a product that will make girls stop doing the math on when they need to wash their hair this week.

It's still in production and if I'm honest, it feels impossible to bring to life sometimes. But we're launching the Instagram on Monday, and I was hoping to use that as an excuse to reach out.

The full story of why I'm doing this to myself is because I'm tired of feeling like I'm not girly enough just because I find it exhausting to do my nails every three weeks while keeping up with skincare every day and eyebrow waxing every other week.

And to be fair, I've felt like this since I was twelve. Like I wasn't working out enough, like I don't take good enough care of my hair, like I don't know enough about makeup or skincare.

Like I'm in a marathon just trying to maintain myself, let alone wanting to be better at it.

Working it out. Somewhere in Madrid.

It got worse over the past two years. I've been living with my boyfriend, moving to a new country every couple of months. Not only was I always next to someone who never needs to make time to maintain himself, I couldn't even gamble on a new eyebrow stylist every time we landed somewhere.

So I started building my own maintenance routine. Partly so I wouldn't get sucked into his routine of washing my hair with dish soap.

(I'm kidding. He's great! He doesn't do that. Anymore....)

✦

Luckily for me, one of our trips led us to Korea, and I really wanted to try the scalp spa everyone on TikTok was talking about.

"For the first time, I didn't need to wash my hair the next day. Or the one after that. Or the one after that...."

When I was looking into that, I found out that apparently it's a thing because most of us leave oil behind when washing our hair. Which btw, "washing our hair", when in reality we really should wash our scalp. The skin and not the hair.

So I looked more into it, and I found a way to copy this routine at home. No one has done that. In the beginning I thought it was because no one understands how annoying it is to be a girl living under her hair.

But now I know it's because it's super complicated to build.

From the looks of it, finding the right manufacturers will take longer than six months. Most of them have never considered that washing your hair less is something women would pay for, or that aesthetics matter. If it doesn't feel good to use, girls won't use it. Full stop.

So I can't keep waiting. On Monday we launch on Instagram, and we're going to start sharing the process of building this as it happens. Not just a product but also a girl's girl company and brand.

✦

I'm building this brand with an incredible creative who has been with me from day one. I noticed she was a girl's girl, and I knew that's what I would need with me. Another girl who won't compromise just because it feels impossible.

Ex-corporate marketing girl. Now lives on a farm with her ducks, sheep, and two dogs. Still a total girly girl with a big collection of sunglasses. She's in South Africa. I'm in Madrid. It works.

Me and Tam, building across continents.

And I would love to have more people around me. So if you want to relive the first chapter of building something, or if you've done this before and want to share advice, or if you just think this is a story worth following, I would love to talk.

I'm especially looking for people who've built something and remember what this pre-launch chapter actually felt like. What would you do differently if you started now?

I'd love to find a way to work together.

Thank you so much for being here and for your time.

romi@lenxi.co
